Marshmallow Test

At this point, you might be wondering what the point of that poorly designed waiting game was about. That was a replica of a well-known test called the Marshmallow Test. The Marshmallow Test is usually conducted on children and the procedure consists of leaving a child alone in a room with a single marshmallow in front of him/her. The child is then told that he/she can eat the marshmallow right now or wait a couple of minutes and the experimenter will bring an additional marshmallow for the child to enjoy. The study showed that about two-thirds of the children either ate the marshmallow right away or took a couple of minutes to think it over and then ate it. On the other hand, the other third of the children waited for the second marshmallow which the experimenter brought in 15 minutes later.

This test studied if having the ability to delay rewards has any correlation with the person's ability to become successful. It turns out that there is a significant difference. In fact, the experimenters surveyed the children once they were in high school and found out that those who waited for the second marshmallow had higher grades, were less stressed out, got accepted to better colleges, and were, in general, better off than those who did not wait for the second marshmallow.

For example, if you look at the charts below, the chart on the left shows the Body Mass Index (BMI) differences between the Non-Delayers (the children who did not wait for the second marshmallow) and the Delayers (the children who waited for the second marshmallow) recorded as a follow-up study years after the subjects were tested as children. It is clear to see that the average BMI differs by about 3 points which is a significant difference. In fact, according to World Health Organization (WHO), 27.5 (Non-Delayers) is considered Overweight and 24.4 (Delayers) is considered Normal/Healthy.

On the other hand, the chart on the right shows the Scholastic Aptitude/Assessment Test (SAT) scores between the Non-Delayers and Delayers. There is about 210 points difference in the total score which adds onto the significance between the difference in Non-Delayers and Delayers.

These two charts, however, are only a part of a large follow-up studies conducted by multiple psychologists throughout the decades. There are other results such as divorce rate, stress level, etc. that demonstrate the better quality of life that the Delayers are experiencing compared to that of the Non-Delayers. So what is it about not eating a marshmallow right away that makes someone successful? The main factor that the Delayers have is the ability to delay instant gratification. The ability to delay instant gratification is important in that those who don't have this ability are victims of a international epidemic: procrastiation.
